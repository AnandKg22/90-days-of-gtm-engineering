# GTM Fundamentals & SaaS Business Models

This guide covers the core Go-To-Market concepts, business models, SaaS economics, and customer journey frameworks essential for a Founding GTM Engineer.

---

## 1. What is Go-To-Market (GTM)?

Go-To-Market is a coordinate strategy that defines how a company reaches, acquires, and retains customers. While traditional software engineering focuses on building the *product*, GTM Engineering focuses on building the *commercial engine* that scales revenue.

| Dimension | Product Engineering | GTM Engineering |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Focus** | Code quality, architecture, scale, latency | Conversion rates, data flow, pipeline speed, RevOps |
| **Users** | External customers | Sales reps, SDRs, AEs, CS, Marketing, Finance |
| **Platforms** | AWS, GCP, Node.js, databases | HubSpot, Salesforce, Stripe, n8n, Segment |
| **Goal** | Build product value | Automate customer acquisition and retain revenue |

## 2. SaaS Business Model & Economics

Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) relies on subscription-based recurring revenue models. As a GTM Engineer, you must master the mathematical formulas driving revenue performance:

### Key Financial Metrics

*   **MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)**: The predictable monthly value of all active subscription contracts.
    $$MRR = \sum (Active \; Subscriptions \times Monthly \; Fee)$$
*   **ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)**: The normalized annual recurring value of subscriptions.
    $$ARR = MRR \times 12$$
*   **ACV (Annual Contract Value)**: The average annual revenue generated by a single customer subscription contract.
*   **LTV (Lifetime Value)**: The total net revenue a customer is expected to pay during their entire contract duration.
    $$LTV = \frac{ARPU \times Gross \; Margin}{Churn \; Rate}$$
    *Where ARPU is Average Revenue Per User/Account.*
*   **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)**: The total cost (sales and marketing spend) required to acquire a single customer.
    $$CAC = \frac{Total \; Sales \; \& \; Marketing \; Costs}{Number \; of \; Customers \; Acquired}$$
*   **CAC Payback Period**: The number of months it takes for a customer to generate enough gross profit to cover their acquisition cost.
    $$Payback \; Period \; (Months) = \frac{CAC}{ARPU \times Gross \; Margin \%}$$
*   **LTV : CAC Ratio**: A measure of sales efficiency.
    *   **Healthy**: $3:1$ or higher (e.g. LTV is 3x CAC).
    *   **Underperforming**: $< 3:1$ (acquiring customers is too expensive).
    *   **Too Conservative**: $> 5:1$ (under-investing in growth).

### Customer & Revenue Retention

*   **Logo Churn Rate**: The percentage of customers who cancel their subscription.
    $$Logo \; Churn \; \% = \frac{Lost \; Customers}{Total \; Customers \; at \; Start} \times 100$$
*   **Gross Revenue Retention (GRR)**: The percentage of recurring revenue retained from existing customers, *excluding* expansion/upsells. Cannot exceed 100%.
    $$GRR \% = \frac{Starting \; MRR - Churn - Contractions}{Starting \; MRR} \times 100$$
*   **Net Revenue Retention (NRR)**: The percentage of recurring revenue retained from existing customers, *including* expansion, upgrades, and cross-selling. Can exceed 100%.
    $$NRR \% = \frac{Starting \; MRR - Churn - Contractions + Expansions}{Starting \; MRR} \times 100$$

## 3. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) & Buyer Persona

A company cannot sell to everyone. GTM Engineering starts with defining exactly *who* to target:

*   **Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)**: A definition of the *organizations* that get the most value from your product and represent the highest LTV. Defined by:
    *   **Firmographics**: Company size, revenue, industry, employee count, location.
    *   **Technographics**: Tech stack used (e.g., uses React, Salesforce, Snowflake).
    *   **Buying Signals**: Recent funding round, hiring trends, leadership changes.
*   **Buyer Persona**: A definition of the *individuals* within those companies who make buying decisions (e.g., VP of Sales, CTO, RevOps Lead). Defined by:
    *   Job title, decision-making authority, daily pain points, KPI goals.

## 4. Market Segmentation

Dividing your total potential market into reachable groups to tailor positioning:
1.  **TAM (Total Addressable Market)**: The total revenue opportunity if you achieved 100% market share.
2.  **SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)**: The portion of the TAM that is targetable by your product and sales model.
3.  **SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)**: The portion of the SAM you can realistically capture in the short term.

## 5. B2B Sales Pipeline Stages

The customer acquisition journey is tracked in a CRM pipeline:

```
Visitor ──> Lead ──> Marketing Qualified (MQL) ──> Sales Qualified (SQL) ──> Opportunity ──> Closed Won (Customer)
```

1.  **Lead**: A contact with basic information (e.g., email) but no validated interest.
2.  **Marketing Qualified Lead (MQL)**: A lead demonstrating engagement (downloaded PDF, visited pricing page).
3.  **Sales Qualified Lead (SQL)**: A lead validated by an SDR as having matching budget, authority, and need.
4.  **Opportunity**: A deal with a clear value size, active discussions, and timeline.
5.  **Closed Won**: Signed contract and onboarding started.
6.  **Closed Lost**: Deal terminated due to competitor, budget, or timing.

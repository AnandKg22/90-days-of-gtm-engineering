# Reflection - Day 001: Introduction to GTM Engineering

A personal log reflecting on the learning outcomes and concepts mastered on Day 1.

---

## 💡 Key Takeaways & Lessons Learned

1.  **Revenue as an Engineering Problem**: This is the most critical shift in mindset. Before today, I thought of sales, marketing, and customer success as "non-technical departments" that run on chats and emails. Today, I realized that these departments run on complex data flows, APIs, and databases. Commercial pipelines can be optimized, unit-tested, and audited just like software applications.
2.  **Silos vs. Integrations**: Startups buy too many software tools (HubSpot, Salesforce, Stripe, Apollo, Clearbit) that remain isolated. The real bottleneck to scaling is the manual effort required to transfer data between these databases. Bridging these gaps is where GTM Engineering excels.
3.  **B2B Customer Journey**: Mapping the complete customer journey from anonymous visitor to paying enterprise customer showed me exactly where data transitions happen. Every transition (e.g. Lead -> SQL or SQL -> Opportunity) is triggered by an event that can be automated.

---

## 🏃 Application to VivaExams

Designing the GTM funnel for VivaExams clarified how to:
*   Identify B2B vs B2C leads instantly by parsing email domain extensions.
*   Automate notifications to Slack so AEs respond within 15 minutes.
*   Integrate Stripe webhooks directly into the mock-exam database to provision seats immediately.
